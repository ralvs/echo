Measured, not guessed. Not alias fragmentation (the Engram and Isabella probes passed), not the 0.5 threshold, not the candidate pool (expanding it moved nothing). The retrieval hypothesis — verdict

Half right: capture-side rot, yes — but the rot was perspective, not aliases.

The July-5 bet was alias fragmentation plus flat results. The stress eval refuted the alias half (Engram and Isabella probes pass) and confirmed a subtler capture-side gap: first-person thoughts never name the Owner, third-person queries always do. The fix was one structural line in the embedding text — “About Renan:” — validated offline with cosine probes before touching the corpus, then confirmed live. The flat-results half remains plausible but unmeasurable until the eval learns to score attached context.

method: 3 read-side knobs tried and measured dead (pool, alpha, threshold) before the write-side fix · every step has a before/after eval run
